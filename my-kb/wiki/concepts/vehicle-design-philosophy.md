# Vehicle Design Philosophy

## Overview
Hyundai's vehicle design philosophy is described as an evolving system rather than a single static look. The company summary names design eras such as Fluidic Sculpture, Cascading Grille, Sensuous Sportiness, and Horizon Lamp, while the Avante summaries show how those ideas appear in concrete model changes.

## From Conservative Completion to Expressive Identity
The 6th-generation Avante AD is framed around practical completion and improved basics. Its slogan-like positioning emphasizes normality, usability, and a stronger product foundation.

The 7th-generation Avante CN7 moves in a more expressive direction. It is described as having a more aggressive exterior, wider stance, lower height, larger wheelbase, and a more digital interior. The CN7 shift shows Hyundai using design not only to modernize a model but also to change the model's image.

## Facelifts as Design Risk
The Avante AD facelift is treated as a major design change with mixed market reaction. CN7 PE also changes lamps, bumper, wheels, and safety/convenience details while keeping the broader interior structure.

Across these summaries, facelifts are not minor cosmetic updates. They are points where Hyundai tests how far it can move a mainstream car's visual identity.
