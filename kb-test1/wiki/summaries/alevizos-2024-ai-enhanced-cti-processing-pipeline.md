# Towards an AI-Enhanced Cyber Threat Intelligence Processing Pipeline

Alevizos and Dekker propose an AI-enhanced CTI processing pipeline for handling the scale, speed, and complexity of modern cyber threats. The paper starts from the observation that traditional CTI workflows rely heavily on manual analyst effort: analysts ingest large volumes of threat data, identify patterns, validate intelligence, contextualize it, recommend mitigations, and verify resilience. This manual model remains valuable but becomes difficult to sustain as threat volume and velocity increase.

The central contribution is a pipeline where AI augments, rather than replaces, human expertise. AI is positioned across the CTI lifecycle: data ingestion, pattern recognition, threat validation, contextual analysis, recommendation generation, and resilience verification. The paper emphasizes that useful CTI requires both computational scale and human judgment. AI can process large datasets, surface correlations, and generate contextual recommendations, while analysts provide domain knowledge, validation, and final decision authority.

The paper also treats AI use in CTI as a governance problem. It highlights ethical dilemmas, bias in both CTI analysis and AI models, transparency requirements, privacy and consent mechanisms, and the risk that the same technology could be misused. The future work agenda includes more advanced AI models for cyber defense and better optimization of human-AI collaboration.
