# CTI for AI Systems

[[summaries/krawczyk-2026-cti-for-ai-systems]] argues that AI systems require CTI frameworks beyond conventional infrastructure-focused threat intelligence. AI assets include data, models, prompts, embeddings, pipelines, deployment environments, and supply-chain artifacts. Attacks may target training, fine-tuning, model hosting, inference, or surrounding application logic.

This idea connects to [[summaries/mavroeidis-2021-cyber-threat-intelligence-model]] because AI-focused CTI needs structured concepts, taxonomies, and ontologies to avoid ambiguous knowledge. It also connects to [[summaries/alevizos-2024-ai-enhanced-cti-processing-pipeline]] because AI can both help process CTI and become the subject of CTI.
