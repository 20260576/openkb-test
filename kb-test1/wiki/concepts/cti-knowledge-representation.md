# CTI Knowledge Representation

[[summaries/mavroeidis-2021-cyber-threat-intelligence-model]] frames CTI as a knowledge representation problem. CTI needs machine-readable structures that can express who, what, why, where, when, and how, plus indicators and courses of action. The paper finds that current ontologies and taxonomies are fragmented and often lack the formal semantics needed for reasoning.

[[summaries/jin-2024-sharing-cyber-threat-intelligence]] shows the operational consequence: STIX data exists, but many objects are narrow, low-level, incomplete, or narrative. [[summaries/krawczyk-2026-cti-for-ai-systems]] adds a new domain where representation has to expand to AI-specific artifacts, vulnerabilities, incidents, and similarity relationships.
