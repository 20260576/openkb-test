# Human-AI Collaboration in CTI

The KB's documents converge on a bounded role for AI in CTI. [[summaries/alevizos-2024-ai-enhanced-cti-processing-pipeline]] argues for human-AI fusion: AI handles scale, correlation, and recommendation generation, while analysts validate and contextualize the intelligence. This model fits CTI because raw data volume is too large for purely manual workflows.

[[summaries/mezzi-2025-llms-unreliable-for-cti]] supplies the cautionary side. If LLMs are inconsistent or overconfident, human review is not optional. CTI outputs affect operational decisions such as patching, prioritization, detection engineering, and incident response. [[summaries/jin-2024-sharing-cyber-threat-intelligence]] shows that even upstream shared data can be incomplete or incorrect, reinforcing the need for analyst judgment before acting on automated outputs.
