# AI-Enhanced CTI Pipeline

[[summaries/alevizos-2024-ai-enhanced-cti-processing-pipeline]] presents the most direct pipeline model: AI assists with ingestion, pattern recognition, contextual analysis, mitigation recommendation, and resilience verification. The goal is faster and more actionable CTI, not full analyst replacement. AI provides scale and speed; human analysts provide validation, domain context, and accountability.

The other documents add constraints to this pipeline. [[summaries/mezzi-2025-llms-unreliable-for-cti]] warns that LLMs may be unreliable when extracting CTI from real reports, so pipeline stages that use LLMs need benchmarking, consistency checks, and confidence calibration. [[summaries/krawczyk-2026-cti-for-ai-systems]] extends the pipeline target: AI itself becomes an asset needing CTI coverage, with model, data, prompt, and supply-chain artifacts requiring their own indicators.
